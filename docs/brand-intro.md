# Adrienne L. Lucas · Brand Walkthrough

Hi Adrienne, we're so excited for you to spend time with this. We put together this one-pager to sit alongside the Figma file as you review, walking you through the thinking behind each decision so the design lands the way it was intended. The thread we kept returning to is that you are not the gatekeeper of your services; you are the person building the rooms and welcoming people into them, and that premise quietly informs every layer of what you'll see. It shows up in the warmth of the palette, in the soft arches on the offering cards, in the editorial cadence of the page, and in the way each section opens with a welcome before it ever asks anything of the visitor. Wherever you land as you scroll, the brand should feel like a space you've prepared, never a wall you've put up.

---

## 1. The color story

- **Cream as the base, not the browns.** A warm cream paper (`#F0E3C8`) sits as the default surface across every section, giving the brand its breathing room and keeping the overall feel polished and inviting rather than dim or heavy. This is the move that lets us honor your warm direction without ever having the page read as "a brown website."
- **A breathing aura where warmth lives.** The richest expression of the palette appears as a slow, breathing gradient layered into the hero background, the three offering card headers, and the footer call-to-action, woven from warm browns and lifted by a deep wine red so it never reads as flat color with a pop of accent. The gradient is in motion on purpose; we tuned it to drift the way breath moves, slow in, slow out, never the same shape twice. That subtle rhythm is what gives the brand its sense of aura and wellness, the feeling of standing in a room that is itself softly inhaling and exhaling around you.
- **Browns and terracotta as anchors and accents, with a quiet move away from cool wellness.** Clay lifts the warm gradient, a deeper wine adds heritage to the offerings, a coffee ink (`#410F0B`) grounds the body type and the wordmark, and a small dose of terracotta sits above section headings where a touch of energy is needed. Together the palette steers wide of the cool blues and sages that the wellness category has saturated, leaving the brand reading warm and grounded, sitting comfortably alongside your stature.

---

## 2. The logo

- **A signature, not a logotype.** The mark is a soft-serif AL monogram drawn the way one signs a name, and that choice was deliberate; a hard, geometric logotype would have signaled brand-as-product, where this brand needed to read as brand-as-person.
- **Inside the same family as the headlines.** The serif voice of the monogram matches the serif used across the site, so the logo sits inside the same language as everything else and never imposes itself on top of the design.
- **A quiet nod to Auria.** Auria carries the meaning of gold and a rare, bright presence, and the mark holds that idea without ever needing to be loud about it; present, not flashy.

---

## 3. The content flow

Each section is positioned to answer a visitor's next likely question, layering positioning, credibility, the work, and proof in a deliberate order.

- **Hero, for positioning.** A visitor immediately sees Adrienne's three roles, the headline, the breathing aura, and a single call to action, so they know exactly who they've found in the first scroll.
- **Press logos, for instant credibility.** A single-ink editorial roll of recognizable press marks (NYT, Cannes Lions, ADCOLOR, and others) sits directly under the hero, confirming the person they've just met is worth their time before they read further.
- **Bio and the three offerings, for context and the work itself.** The bio establishes her background and credentials, and the three offerings (Auria Creative Well, Talent & Connections, Dialogue) follow with the throughline above them showing how the three connect.
- **Lifestyle photography, recognition, and brand roll, for layered credibility.** A short lifestyle pause shows the environments and events she has built, leading into the recognition timeline of her track record and the brand roll of companies she has worked with.
- **Testimonials and the closing call to action.** Testimonials sit near the end as the most direct form of proof, in the words of the people she has worked with, and the page closes on the throughline it opened with, paired with a final invitation to get in touch.
